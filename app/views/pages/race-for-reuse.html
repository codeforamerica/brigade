Apps that are Easy, Important, and Even Fun to Launch

Adopta↓

A web app that encourages citizens to "adopt" civic infrastructure, such as fire hydrants, storm drains, trees, emergency sirens...anything, really. Adopta has already been deployed in 4 cities -- your city can be next.

Your objective is to maximize adoptions and see them through when action is needed. This might be a good app for you if:

  • You have Ruby on Rails expertise.
  • You have a dataset you want to use.

Get started with Adopta →

Textizen ↓

A feedback tool for the mobile age, with SMS. Questions about the city landscape, ongoing events, etc, and get realtime input from people in the community.

This might be a good app for you if: You are a city official or have contacts in your local government. You don’t have a technical background. You have a specific question(s) or project for which you want feedback from your community. Your objective is to organize a poll for your city and publish results in public.

  • It's trivial to launch. Fill out the "Textizen in your city" form on www.textizen.com. Let them know you are participating in the Code for America App Raiser campaign.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time making and promoting a poll that's relevant in your city.

Get started with Textizen →

LocalWiki ↓

A platform for building community information hubs. LocalWikis are wikis about a place written by the people of that place.

This might be a good app for you if: You have a local group who wants to be involved to help seed content. You like community organizing. Your objective is to maximize contributions and links to your wiki.

  • Launch: It's trivial. Just send email to LocalWiki & let them know you want an instance for the Code for America campaign.
  • Make Useful: You'll need to invest time recruiting authors and curators. Take a look at how they did it at TriangleWiki

Get started with LocalWiki →

Shareabouts ↓

Shareabouts is a mapping tool to gather crowd sourced public input. Use it to collect suggested locations and comments, in a social, engaging process.

Shareabouts is ideal for crowdsourcing bikeshare stations and gathering input on wayfinding projects. .

  • gather suggestions for possible locations for bike share stations
  • get comments and votes on proposed locations for food carts
  • collect data on vacant lots that could be urban farms
  • show infrastructure options and get public comment on each one
  • collect support for possible park locations
  • crowdsource issue reporting after a storm

Get started with Shareabouts →
