1. Overview

As you learned on the Brigade About page and Brigade Organize page , Brigades are local volunteer groups that bring together community members to help make government work better - and they exist all over the world! No matter their location, Brigades build technology to support local government accountability & civic engagement.

This page will dig deeper into all the nuts and bolts you need to know if you are organizing a Brigade in the US or Internationally.

2014 Code for America Fellow Tiffany Chu discusses civic hacking with the Code for Charlotte Brigade

You can see where Brigades are already located on the Brigade Website Map . It is best to concentrate Brigade work with one central Brigade in each location, although some Brigades participate as Regional Brigades, and invite residents from neighboring cities to participate until that city is ready to have their own Brigade. We can help you figure out what makes sense as you get started.

If you are pretty sure no one is organizing in your area, let's get started! The first thing you should know is that Brigades can participate as Network Members or Official Chapter; and the distinction is embedded within our organizing structre, the four Ps. Right now we are going to go into to depth with each P, but you can skip to the checklist.

*Note: US Brigades can join as Official Chapters and Network Members, International Brigades are invited to join as Network Members and can then explore becoming a Code for All partner.

2. People

We believe government has to work for everyone; so our aim to get as many people involved so our Brigades are rich with all the different skills sets your entire community has to offer. Afterall, the members of your community are what will power your Brigade - so you should invite everyone to join!

So who is that exactly? Technologists, researchers, public policy enthusiasts, committed residents, city employees, and everyone in between!

  1. You can ensure people's safety by implementing a Code of Conduct; fork this one!
  2. Next, build a website! Your Brigade needs a landing page so people can find you, plus you need a place where you can point to your Code of Conduct.
    3. -Your website can be something you build, can be a public Facebook or Meetup Account, or a page you fork using some base code Code for San Francisco made.
    -You can then use your website to advertise your event schedule...
  3. Start meeting! Find some space, head to the local library, ask your boss if you can use your office, etc. You should meet at least three times before you apply to become a Network Member, and hosting a CodeAcross or National Day event counts. If you are an existing group with lots of meetings under your belt that works, too.
  4. As people start to show up to your meetings (which we call hack nights) you can help folks get more involved by offering Core Team Member roles (the roles in this doc capture the big bucket to-dos of any Brigade, but you can hack the names and add more leadership roles to your squad). Having Core Team Members helps you delegate responsibilities, and finally get some sleep.

Code for America Headquarters, San Francisco, Ca.

*Note:If you complete this step, your ready to become a Network Member, you just need to fill out the "Join as a Brigade Network Member" form and adhere to the "Participate" activities below.

3. Participate

Hearing from you and sharing recent activities with the Code for America network will allow your Brigade to fruitfully grow and sustain.

  1. As a Brigade Network Member we will make it easy for you to connect with other organizers through our:
    2. - Brigade Slack Channel
    - Brigade Map
    - forum.codeforamerica.org
    - … and other mediums we use to swap stories, ideas, and plans for global network events, like…
  2. Collective Days of Action are also ways you will participate in the network. Your Brigade will want to participate in at least two collective days of action per year, for example CodeAcross and National Day of Civic Hacking is June 6! Register now!)
  3. Tell us over here at San Francisco’s Headquarters what’s up! You can fill out this “What’s New with Your Brigade” survey anytime to share a cool new story or project.

Code for Long Beach at CodeAcross 2014

*Note:Once you have People, fill out the "Join as a Brigade Network form", and Participate in the above channels that we will invite you to, you are a Brigade Network Member. As your grow you may consider the following two Ps, which allow you to evolve into an Brigade Official Chapter.

4. Plans

You know all those ideas that have been circulating at hack night? We want to help you put those into action!

  1. You should use this Strategic Plan Template to outline your goals and consider how you will measure outcomes.
  2. You will also need to orient your plans around the Principles for 21st Century Government and the Code for America Focus Areas; so plan to bucket your goals in the above template around these elements.
  3. Also, within the template you will link to a Budget Tracker which can help us both plan, monitor, and reimburse your Brigade expenses.

Post-its are great for making plans!

5. Partnerships

Since you started organizing, all kinds of folks have probably been encouraging you and mentioning organizations they know of or belong to that would support the work of your Brigade - so you should partner with them!

  1. To become an Official Brigade you will need at least two partnerships in place:
    2. - one with a local community organization (NGOs, school, etc.) that has data, talent, or space to share
    Examples: a Community Health clinic with data it can share, the local police department, the local library, a code-teaching organization (like Girl Develop It).
    - and another with a local government employee.
    Examples: Your city's Cheif Data Officer, and employee in the City Manager's Office, a representative of the County Innovation office, etc.
  2. Collaborating with these folks will make it a lot easier to implement that awesome plan of yours, and once your have a few partners you will notice more and more keep rolling in.

*Note:If you complete all four ps, your ready to become an Official Chapter! You just need to cross check the checklist below, then fill out the "Join as a Brigade Official Chapter" form.

6. Checklist

To join us as Brigade Network Member

Get your PEOPLE in order, and PARTICIPATE

  1. Create a Code of Conduct
  2. Create a website
  3. Meet at least three times
  4. Assign Leadership roles
  5. Fill in the Brigade Network Member submission form
  6. Use Slack, the forum.codeforamerica.org, this survey, email, etc. to share and ask questions

Code for America event swag

To join us as Brigade Official Chapter

Get your PEOPLE in order, and PARTICIPATE, make PLANS, and get some PARTNERS

  1. Do all the things noted in the “Join as a Brigade Network Member” checklist
  2. Create a Strategic Plan including a Budget
  3. Establish a partnership with another community organization
  4. Establish a partnership with someone who works for your city our county
  5. Fill in the Official Brigade Chapter submission form

7. Resources

These are the basic resources for Brigade Network Members

  1. Access to the Code for America Brigade Slack channel
  2. Workshops and trainings (find a list on the forum.codeforamerica.org)
  3. Access to the Brigade Support Request form
  4. Super awesome free tools!
  5. Our undivided personal attention whenever you need: brigade-info@codeforamerica.org

These are the basic resources for Brigade Official Chapters

  1. All the cool things Brigade Network Members receive
  2. Access to up to $5,500 in funding for 2015
  3. A free invitation to our Annual Code for America Summit (two tickets per Brigade)
  4. A free Code for America Track Jacket (1 per Brigade Captain)
  5. Even more free tools!
